Dancing in Queens is Thriving. Advocates Say Space and City Funding Have Fallen Short.

October 11, 2023 | By Kimberly Izar

QUEENS — Dancers hit the stage for the Queensboro Dance Festival last September, with over two dozen companies from all corners of Queens. But advocates say bringing together dancers in the biggest borough hasn't been so easy.

“Queens is the biggest borough. It's the most diverse borough," said Karesia Batan, founder of the Queensboro Dance Festival, a summer tour of performances and classes based in Queens.

"There must be stuff happening here that, but why don't we know about it?”

Advocates named inaccessible transportation, premium-priced rehearsal space, and disproportionate borough funding as reasons that Queens have been left on the arts hub outskirts.

For dancers in Queens, the odds can feel especially stacked against them.

LACK OF FUNDING



Out of 125 organizations that received grants from the city’s Cultural Development Fund in the current fiscal year, nearly half of those grantees were from Manhattan, and only 12% were from Queens.

ZONING RESTRICTIONS

Zoning laws also prohibit legal dancing in specific zoning jurisdictions depending on capacity maximums.

In 2017, New York City repealed the Prohibition-era Cabaret Law, which required businesses to own a cabaret license. Some advocates say that regulations that restrict dancing in areas outside of manufacturing and commercial spaces still exist.

SHORTAGE OF SPACE

Majority of subsidized rehearsal spaces offered by the city are based in Manhattan, leaving outer boroughs like Staten Island and Queens with few affordable rehearsal hubs.

Dance/NYC offers subsidized rehearsal space throughout the city, though only two out of 20 venues are based in Queens.
